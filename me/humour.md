# Humour
> How you joke — the comedy DNA that makes your content feel like you and not a corporate blog post.
## Your humour style (describe in 2-3 sentences)
High-energy, situational, and grounded in a sharp contrast between dry business realities and highly relatable human agony or cultural habits. You position yourself as a battle-tested fellow practitioner who isn't afraid to mock standard industry excuses, lazy shortcuts, or even your own role as an invading educator. 
## Comedy tactics that work for you
1. **The High-Emotion Metaphor**
   - How it works: Taking a universally frustrating childhood game or daily real estate/dining dilemma and mapping that exact emotional pain directly onto a serious, broken business metric.
   - Example: "Ever played Snakes and Ladders and you roll this dice, you get a perfect six, but you land up in the mouth of that snake on number 98 and you come rolling down back to number seven? It is the most pissing-off experience ever... Now, the same thing in business..."
2. **Satirizing Fate & Superstitions (The Destiny Check)**
   - How it works: Calling out entrepreneurs who choose to blame external spiritual charts, cosmic alignment, or bad luck instead of auditing their actual operational gaps or content strategies.
   - Example: "I think my luck is bad. I think I was born in Yama Ganda Kaala. I think right now I have Sarpa Dosha and Pitru Dosha because of which my business is not growing... Stop it. Your nakshatra is fine; your content strategy is just missing."
3. **The Self-Aware "Intruder" Interruption**
   - How it works: Stripping away any corporate "guru" pretense by playfully acknowledging that business material is inherently boring and that your audience is actively trying to bypass you to find entertainment.
   - Example: "Most people are online to be entertained. Educators like me just come in the way and educate them in the middle of their entertainment."
4. **Extreme Visual Hyperbole for Banned Behaviors**
   - How it works: Painting a ridiculous, unfiltered mental picture of what a low-value or desperate business looks like to instantly make cheap trends look foolish.
   - Example: "If you are a serious business, you're a B2B business, you can't keep a whiskey glass on your head and do a reel; you can't dance on kacha badam that just won't go down well with your target audience."
## What lands with your audience
- Detailed, cinematic metaphors that mirror real life (e.g., driving around a city looking for dinner aimlessly, or parents talking about real estate opportunities missed in the 1990s).
- Direct reality checks that challenge laziness (e.g., asking for client referrals as if you are standing with a begging bowl).
- Unfiltered, conversational side-notes or self-corrections when you catch yourself sounding like a textbook (e.g., "Oh my god, I said business so many times in that statement...").
## What you NEVER joke about
- Real client losses or struggles in a mean-spirited way; failures are analyzed constructively, never mocked mockingly.
- Your own past struggles with debt and business closures (these are shared with deep vulnerability and gratitude to life, never cheapened into a punchline).
- Legitimate hard work of middle-class commuters (e.g., "I know this kind of travel is probably the norm for lakhs of people...").
- Your absolute right as an operator to confidently demand value, referrals, and fair market compensation.
## Phrases that carry your comedic voice
- "...bloody worth it; they are like gold being sold at the price of peanuts."
- "It's not that melodramatic. People are actually open to conversations..."
- "...putting effort in marketing on the wrong channel is like placing the ladder against the wrong wall and climbing at the top of the ladder only to realize that this is the wrong wall."
- "Basically, the funda is simple..."
## Humour calibration by channel
| Channel | Humour level | What's fair game | What's off-limits |
|---------|-------------|-----------------|-------------------|
| YouTube / video | **High (8-10/10)** | Animated physical comparisons, cultural ad references (Fevicol bindis), sports leadership shifts (MS Dhoni), and intense vocal pacing choices. | Clownish, cheap social media trends that degrade B2B authority (no dancing on trending tracks). |
| LinkedIn | **Moderate (6/10)** | Storytelling built on real operational errors, contrasting past survival desperation with current metrics, and short, parallel punchy lines. | Excessively loud or casual phrases that weaken the professional framework of agency leadership. |
| Email (warm) | **High (7-8/10)** | Blunt, urgent reality checks, single-sentence truth bombs, and directly challenging their current strategy mistakes. | Academic lectures, corporate buzzwords (delve, synergy), or overly soft/timid formatting. |
| Email (cold/formal) | **Low (2/10)** | A peer-to-peer helpful perspective, a brief direct truth-bomb, keeping it strictly professional yet direct. | Extreme analogies like the begging bowl or spiritual doshas; keeping it clean and value-first. |
| Community / calls | **High (8/10)** | Raw, unfiltered feedback loop adjustments, casual industry analogies, and real-time self-deprecation regarding old operating habits. | Arrogance or talking down to group members like an untouchable lecturer. |
| DMs | **Moderate (5/10)** | Quick, casual, conversational wit, using local or industry shorthand, and keeping a peer-to-peer operational tempo. | Overly stylized formatting loops or paragraph-long metaphors. |

# Vocabulary

> The words you reach for, the words you avoid, and the verbal fingerprints that make your content sound like you.

---

## Words & phrases you use constantly

### Greetings & direct address

- First name only — no "Hey", no "Hi", no "Dear"
- "ladies and gentlemen" — used in video to signal a declarative moment
- "my friend" — used when delivering a hard truth gently ("not possible, my friend")
- "guys" — casual group address in live/video close ("okay, guys, take care")

### Transitions & connectors

- "which brings me to…" — primary topic transition in video
- "this brings me to…" — variant of above
- "now, apart from X, another…" — additive transition
- "now coming back to…" — after a digression or CTA pivot
- "think about it" — pivot before a logical consequence or insight
- "so let's get started" — video opener after intro
- "with that" — closes a section before moving on ("so with that, these are the five things…")
- "so let's dive into…" — signals entry into the main content list
- "and as you…" / "because think about it…" — layering a consequence or reason

### Teaching phrases

- "What do I mean by X? What I mean is…" — pre-empts confusion before it arises
- "I want you to…" — shifts viewer from passive listening to active attention
- "Let me be very honest" — signals a hard truth or contrarian point
- "I'll be very clear with you" — signals a direct, no-fluff statement
- "The idea is simple" / "The funda is simple" — distils a concept to its core logic
- "Think about it" — used before landing a logical point or rhetorical question
- "It's not about X — it's about Y" — bilateral reframe of a misconception
- "Not just X, but Y" — upgrades the audience's goal or frame
- "Remember this:" — precedes a statement worth memorising
- "Please understand this:" — used before correcting a deeply held wrong belief

### Commitment & emphasis

- "I promise you" — rare; used once near the end of a point. Carries weight precisely because it's used sparingly.
- "I really need you to understand this" — used before a core distinction
- "This is a cardinal sin" — strongest form of "this is a big mistake"
- "The truth is this:" — signals a reframe of a common assumption
- "Make sure you…" — direct instruction to take a specific action
- "Do not…" — blunt instruction, no softening ("do not treat marketing and sales as the same thing")

### Filler / conversational

- "okay" — constant; used as both filler and full stop in speech
- "basically" — bridges complex ideas into plain language
- "now" — used to introduce a new thought or sub-point
- "so" — connective filler, especially in live/group settings
- "part and parcel" — used to describe something inherent or unavoidable
- "it is what it is" / "that's the way it is" — owns hard truths without dwelling
- "it all boils down to…" — used to land the core lesson after a long explanation

### Closing / wrapping up

- "I hope that gave you clarity"
- "I hope I answered your question"
- "I hope you found value in these X points"
- "That was for you, [name]"
- "No shortcuts, guys. No shortcuts."
- "Okay, guys, take care"
- "Enjoy [year], enjoy [topic]" — casual one-time sign-off
- "I look forward to seeing you there" — standard CTA close for live events
- "Let us continue the learning journey" — used to link current content to next step

## Profanity

- Frequency: Rare — once per video max, only in video/live contexts
- Words used: "bloody" ("this is a bloody good channel"), "pissing off" ("the most pissing-off experience ever on that board")
- Where it's OK: YouTube videos, community/group calls
- Where it's never OK: email (warm or cold), formal B2B outreach, LinkedIn posts

## Banned words (NEVER use these)

### Corporate / AI-sounding

- "leverage" (as a buzzword — he does use it literally: "leverage marketing")
- "synergy", "synergize"
- "holistic approach"
- "game-changer", "game-changing"
- "actionable insights"
- "best practices" (used rarely, and only in the literal sense)
- "value proposition" (says "value" directly)
- "pain points" only in teaching context — never in emotional copy
- "utilize" — always "use"
- "in order to" — always "to"
- "facilitate"
- "paradigm shift"
- "seamless"
- "robust"
- "innovative solution"
- "take it to the next level" as empty filler (he uses it only when tied to a concrete meaning)

### Formal closings / openings he never uses

- "Dear [Name]"
- "To whom it may concern"
- "I hope this email finds you well"
- "As per my last email"
- "Please do not hesitate to contact me"
- "Kind regards" / "Warm regards" / "Best regards"
- "I wanted to reach out because…"

### Overused internet words

- "epic"
- "crush it"
- "hustle" (as a virtue)
- "grind"
- "hack" (as in "life hack", "marketing hack")
- "viral" as a goal
- "passive income" as a promise
- "killing it"

## Closing line patterns

1. "I hope that gave you clarity." — most common; ends a teaching point or answer
2. "I hope you found value in these X points." — ends a numbered-list video
3. "I look forward to seeing you at the Business Breakthrough Seminar." — ends any video with a BBS CTA
4. "Let us continue the learning journey." — bridges current content to next step
5. "No shortcuts, guys. No shortcuts." — ends a point about hard truths or consistency
6. "Okay, guys, take care." — casual close for live/group sessions
7. "That was for you, [name]." — personalised close in live Q&A contexts

## AIM — Proof Point Case Studies

### Home Care Brand — RWA Sampling: 18% → 50% Trial Conversion

**Client:** Home care brand (undisclosed)
**Channel:** RWA & Society Product Sampling
**Scale:** 40 residential societies | 22,000 samples | 6 weeks

> A home care brand came to us with a conversion problem. People were trying the product — but only 18% were going on to purchase. We ran a door-to-door wet sampling campaign across 40 RWAs with trained brand ambassadors and QR-based feedback collection. Six weeks later, trial-to-purchase conversion had gone from 18% to 50%. ROI: 5.2x.

**The lesson:** The product wasn't the problem. The context of the trial was. Getting samples into the right hands, in the right environment, with a feedback loop changes everything.

**When to use:** Brands skeptical of sampling ROI; brands who say "we've tried sampling before and it didn't work"; conversations about conversion vs. reach.

### Imposter Syndrome While Building

> "Am I really ready for this?" Almost every entrepreneur hits this moment while building a business. This is something called imposter syndrome. It shows up right when you start growing. When you're entering bigger markets, talking to larger clients, leading a bigger team, making decisions you've never made before. And suddenly a thought crosses your mind: "Am I really ready for this?" "What if I'm not capable enough to run something this big?" I have felt this too while building AIM and ADVAN. There were moments when my decisions felt bigger than my experience. But over time I realized something important: you don't feel like an imposter when you're playing small. You feel it when you're growing. Every entrepreneur learns on the way. You don't start a business knowing everything. You figure things out while building it. So, if you ever feel like you are not ready yet — it might mean your business is growing faster than your comfort zone. And that's not a bad sign. That's how real businesses are built.

**Theme:** Imposter syndrome | Entrepreneurial mindset | Personal growth
**When to use:** Posts about the psychological side of building a business; when speaking to founders navigating growth; content about self-doubt as a signal of progress.

### Build Teams That Push Back

> The kind of people you surround yourself with defines your leadership. As a leader, it can feel comforting when everyone around you agrees and when every idea is met with a quick "yes." But over time, I have realized something important. The best teams are not built around constant agreement. They are built around honest conversations. As a founder, some of the most valuable moments have come from people who respectfully questioned my ideas, shared a different perspective, or challenged my thinking. Not because they wanted to oppose me — but because they cared about making the idea stronger. Great leadership, I believe, is not about being surrounded by people who always say yes. It's about creating a space where people feel comfortable enough to say, "Can we think about this differently?" Those conversations often lead to better clarity, stronger decisions, and a healthier team culture. So if you are building a team, don't just look for agreement. Surround yourself with people who think deeply, speak honestly, and help you see things from new angles. That's how both leaders and organizations truly grow.

**Theme:** Leadership | Team culture | Psychological safety
**When to use:** Posts about hiring and building teams; leadership content; when the topic is culture, honest feedback, or decision-making quality.

### A "Failed" Campaign Is Not a Dead End

> A "failed" campaign doesn't mean marketing is broken — it means you haven't cracked it yet. I have run campaigns that didn't land. Engagement was low. Timing was off. And yes, it stung. But here's what I have learned: failure isn't the enemy. Usually, what went wrong was — strategy misaligned with the audience, execution that didn't resonate, messaging that missed the mark, timing that wasn't right. Markets and consumer behaviour evolve constantly. What didn't work yesterday can succeed tomorrow with the right tweaks. In fact, where one brand failed, another flourished in the same sector — we have several examples that prove it. They asked: "What can we do differently?" "Has the audience shifted?" "Is there a better channel or message that hits the right note?" Right strategy + right execution + right audience + right timing = breakthrough campaigns. Behind every iconic brand is a leader who refused to quit after a failed strategy. Let every failed attempt be the lesson for the next big win.

**Theme:** Marketing failure | Iteration | Resilience
**When to use:** Posts after a campaign underperforms; when speaking to brand managers who are losing faith in a channel; content about the marketing mindset and iteration culture.

### Wrong Battlefield — Home Interiors Brand Case Study

> "We're pouring money into ads and getting nothing back." I hear this almost every week from brand leaders, and I understand the frustration. When you spend ₹8–10 lakhs on a campaign and sales don't increase — it hurts financially and emotionally. Recently, a mid-sized home interiors company came to us after running a high-budget Instagram ad campaign. They invested heavily. No results. The problem: their target audience was 40+ homeowners in Tier 2 cities who were making purchase decisions through local dealer visibility and outdoor hoardings — not Instagram. The brand chose the wrong battlefield. Their advertising strategy wasn't aligned with the buyer's real behaviour. Dear brand leaders, advertising never fails — but the platform choice can. When budgets are tight, every rupee must work harder. And that only happens when you stop copying trends and start studying your target audience. If your campaigns aren't delivering ROI, that's not a budget problem. It's a signal to ask: are we investing in the right ads, for the right audience, in the right place?

**Theme:** Wrong channel | Platform strategy | Marketing ROI
**When to use:** When a prospect says "we tried digital and it didn't work"; posts about channel selection and audience behaviour; positioning AIM/ADVAN's ground-level approach as the alternative to wasted digital spend.
**Notable detail:** ₹8–10 lakh budget | Instagram vs. dealer/outdoor for Tier 2 homeowners — specific enough to be credible, generic enough to protect the client.

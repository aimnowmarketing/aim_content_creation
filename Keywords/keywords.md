## Keyword usage rules

1. **Use one primary keyword per page or post.**
   - Example: `product sampling agency in India`

2. **Use 3-6 secondary keywords naturally.**
   - Example: `product sampling services`, `digital product sampling`, `in-store product sampling`, `sampling campaign ROI`

3. **Match keyword to intent.**
   - Service keywords belong on landing pages.
   - Question keywords belong in blogs, guides, FAQs, and explainers.
   - Competitor keywords belong in comparison pages, PPC planning, and internal research — not aggressive client-facing copy.

4. **Never write like a keyword robot.**
   - Bad: “AIM is a product sampling agency in India offering product sampling services in India for product sampling campaigns in India.”
   - Better: “If your brand wants consumers to actually try the product before judging it, AIM helps you run measurable product sampling campaigns across India.”

5. **Do not use high-volume consumer keywords blindly.**
   - Keywords like `free product samples` may bring traffic, but most of that traffic is from consumers looking for freebies, not brands looking for a sampling partner.

6. **The real buyer is a brand-side decision maker.**
   - Write for the person who controls budget, launch plans, GTM campaigns, or field marketing.
